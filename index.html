01 / The operational challenge

Complex work deserves a connected operating picture.

Critical context often lives across status reports, spreadsheets, contracts, email and individual knowledge. MRMS brings it together without removing the professional judgment aviation work demands.

Fragmented inputs create operational exposure.

The problem is not the expertise of the teams. It is the effort required to reconcile changing technical and financial information at scale.

  • 01Distributed informationAircraft and component data spread across reports, email and departments.
  • 02Contract complexityVariable rates, escalation periods, caps and calculation bases.
  • 03Missed opportunityReimbursable events and workscope follow-up can become difficult to track.
  • 04Limited traceabilityManual reconciliation obscures the source, rule and reviewer behind a decision.
  • 05Knowledge dependencyRepeated entry and reminders depend heavily on individual continuity.

02 / What MRMS is

An integrated aviation lifecycle and maintenance-reserve management platform.

MRMS converts operational data into controlled engineering and financial workflows—carrying context from first input through final evidence.

  3. 03Traceable calculations and decisions
  4. 04Early identification of reimbursable events
  5. 05Management-ready visibility and exports

03 / Platform capabilities

One platform. The full reserve lifecycle.

Explore the functional areas that connect fleet configuration, reserve accounting, maintenance activity and governance.

A/C01 — ASSET CONTEXT

Fleet & Component Management

A searchable, accountable view of aircraft, titled components, installation states and movement history.

  • Registration, MSN, lessor and handler records
  • Serial-number configuration and title periods
  • Installed, removed, spare and in-shop states

Track delivery and redelivery, physical versus contractual assignment, responsibility, aircraft timelines and complete component movement history—with validation against overlapping title periods.

MX02 — OPERATIONS

Maintenance Event Control

Synchronize component movements, shop visits, hangar induction and reimbursement workflows.

  • TSN/CSN at installation and removal
  • Shop induction, completion and release
  • Structural check milestones

Capture time and cycles since installation, six-, eight- and twelve-year checks, and connect confirmed events directly to relevant claims.

∑03 — CALCULATION

Maintenance Reserve Calculation

Produce consistent monthly calculations across aircraft, component and custom contractual reserve items.

  • FHR, cycles and monthly calculation bases
  • Caps, adjustments and cumulative balances
  • Jet and turboprop reserve structures

Calculate beginning and ending utilization, monthly differences, under/overpayment, net payable, annualized utilization and sector-time ratios across engine, LLP, APU, landing gear, propeller and structural reserves.

ƒx04 — CONTRACT LOGIC

Rates, Forms & Escalation

Translate aircraft-specific contractual terms into controlled, period-effective calculation logic.

  • Effective periods and decimal precision
  • Interpolation, severity and derate logic
  • Date-controlled escalation validation

Configure lessor and lease references, mappings, assumptions, reserve caps and custom items. Required escalation gaps stop calculations rather than creating silent ambiguity.

PDF05 — DATA INGESTION

Utilization Report Processing

Convert monthly aircraft status reports into identified, reviewable calculation inputs.

  • PDF parsing of aircraft FHR and CYC
  • Component-specific extraction and matching
  • Source evidence and controlled reparse

Support varying layouts, compare previous and current month values, identify fallback sources and preserve the evidence used by each calculation.

✓✓06 — ASSURANCE

Review, Validation & Exports

Move from extracted value to signed, management-ready evidence through staged control.

  • Review-before-save and two-level validation
  • PDF, Excel, ledgers and approval packages
  • Snapshots, cautions and signatures

Validated decisions are never silently replaced. Export scopes and warnings make validation status visible across aircraft reports, monthly packages and lessor approval letters.

RMB07 — FINANCIAL RECOVERY

Workscope & Reimbursement

Maintain continuity from draft workscope and lessor review through active claim and conclusion.

  • Contribution and accrual channels
  • Maximum contribution and accrued reserves
  • Overlap checks and urgency indicators

Manage aircraft and component claims, separate engine reimbursement intervals, attachments, responsible handlers and awaiting-CRS, active and concluded states.

10D08 — EARLY WARNING

Maintenance & Movement Alerts

Surface upcoming reimbursable work and component events for deliberate human action.

  • Ten Days maintenance-plan alerts
  • Removal/installation report parsing
  • Record, reject, ignore and restore controls

Preserve completed decisions across plan refreshes. Critical events remain pending until a reviewer confirms parsed values, system state and downstream action.

IN09 — INFORMATION CONTROL

Notification Center & Governance

Classify incoming reports, route exceptions and govern platform continuity.

  • Duplicate and unknown-report staging
  • Notifications, delivery records and retry
  • Controlled backup and recovery audit

Retain mailbox metadata, processing logs, configurable distributions and full status history while role-aware actions and validated recovery protect system integrity.
